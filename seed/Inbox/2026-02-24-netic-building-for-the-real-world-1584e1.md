# Netic: Building for the Real World

*By Melisa Tokmak · February 24, 2026*

Silicon Valley obsesses over AI copilots for coders and tools for other tech companies. Melisa Tokmak grew up in Salihli, Turkey, attended Stanford on scholarship, and noticed her peers chased "sexy problems" — developer tools, crypto — while ignoring the industries that keep the physical world running.

Netic targets the real-world economy: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, automotive. Essential services with razor-thin margins, high employee churn, and no well-funded software champion.

Two years in, 32 people, mission-critical customers.

## Five principles Tokmak lives by

1. **Real-world problems matter because they're hard.** Difficulty is a moat.
2. **Critical infrastructure is resilient long-term.** Customers don't churn when your software runs their business.
3. **Success requires being in the room.** Operational excellence and direct customer engagement, not Zoom calls.
4. **Meaningful work attracts great people.** Purpose compounds.
5. **Sustainable impact requires profit.** No growth theatre.

The $500B+ essential services market is largely untouched by well-capitalised software companies — Netic is going after it.
